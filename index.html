CraftyJet™ Brand Guidelines

Welcome to the brand guidelines for CraftyJet™, a game-changing compact DTF printer designed to empower home crafters and hobbyists alike. Designed with precision and purpose for the crafting market, this printer brings professional-grade quality into home studios or tight workspaces, without compromising on vibrancy, reliability or performance.
These guidelines define how to present CraftyJet™ confidently and consistently across every touchpoint, ensuring the innovation and accessibility of the brand is always reflected.


Updated: October 2025

Logo

Design & Legibility

The CraftyJet™ logo is designed to remain clear at all display sizes. Use the official artwork—never redraw, distort, recolour, or add effects.
No maximum size, but minimum size is 20px (screen) and ¼ inch (print). If legibility is lost, it's too small.

On Colour

Use only on White, Cerulean, Black, School Bus Yellow, Rose, or Oslo Gray backgrounds for clarity.

Clearspace

Leave a margin at least the height of the 'C' in CraftyJet™ (x) on all sides. No text or graphics should invade this area.

Clearspace = x-height of 'C'

Logo Usage Don'ts

Typography

Primary Typeface

Montserrat is the cornerstone of CraftyJet™ visual identity—modern, geometric, and highly versatile for headlines, subheadings, labels, packaging, and body text. All CraftyJet™ touchpoints (print and digital) must use Montserrat for clarity and consistency.

Montserrat ABCDEFGHIJKLMNOPQRSTUVWXYZ
abcdefghijklmnopqrstuvwxyz 0123456789

Download Montserrat Font

Weights

  • Thin (100): Premium packaging accents, oversized display text.
  • Regular (400): Body copy, paragraph text, documentation.
  • Medium (500): Section headings, subheads, strong interface text.
  • Bold (700): Main headings and titles for strong impact.
  • ExtraBold (800): Reserved for maximum attention (hero graphics, banners).

Don’ts

  • Do not use fonts other than Montserrat.
  • No decorative/script fonts or all-uppercase for body text.

Printing Guide

  • Always use the CMYK colour values supplied in this document for any physical print.
  • Export artwork as PDF/X-1a, TIFF, or EPS for print production; do not use standard RGB files for print output.
  • Ask your print provider if any custom adjustments are needed for print conditions.
  • Preview with Pantone swatches if required for spot colour printing.
  • For highest fidelity, lock vector format and embed fonts in exported files.
